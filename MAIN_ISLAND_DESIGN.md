# Main Island Design Specifications

## Overview
This document outlines the detailed specifications for the main island within "The Great War of Land". It covers the city layout, districts, landmarks, and environmental details, providing a comprehensive vision for the development of the island.

## City Layout
- **Central Hub**: The main city is located at the center of the island, featuring a circular design with radial streets radiating outwards. This allows for efficient traffic flow and accessibility to all districts.
- **Zones**: The city will be divided into several zones:
  - Residential Zone: Housing for inhabitants, including multi-family units and single-family homes.
  - Commercial Zone: Shops, markets, and dining establishments located around the central hub.
  - Industrial Zone: Factories and warehouses situated at the periphery to minimize pollution in residential areas.

## Districts
- **Historical District**: This area includes museums, libraries, and preserved buildings that reflect the island's history and culture.
- **Cultural District**: Areas designated for art galleries, theaters, and performance spaces to promote local artists and cultural events.
- **Natural District**: Parks and green spaces integrated into the city layout, offering recreational opportunities and preserving local flora and fauna.

## Landmarks
- **The Great Monument**: A towering structure in the center of the central hub commemorating the island's heroes and history.
- **The Grand Canal**: A waterway that runs through the city, providing both aesthetic beauty and practical transportation routes.
- **Sky Gardens**: Rooftop gardens on commercial buildings designed to enhance biodiversity and provide community gathering spaces.

## Environmental Details
- **Climate**: The island experiences a temperate climate, with moderate rainfall and four distinct seasons, influencing the types of flora and fauna present.
- **Sustainability Initiatives**: Efforts to utilize renewable energy, such as solar panels and wind turbines, along with waste recycling programs and organic community gardens.
- **Wildlife Preservation**: Designated conservation areas for local wildlife and habitats, ensuring that development does not encroach on sensitive environmental zones.
